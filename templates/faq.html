FAQ

What is this site?

Diddydonation.com is specifically for really small donations (one penny) that you give to a webpage if you find it interesting, useful or fun.

What is the point of such a small donation?

  • A 1p donation is such a small commitment that you don't need to justify it to yourself - just do it!
  • It's a great way to show your support for a website - Put your money where your mouth is!
  • We believe it's more likely for 1000 people to donate 1p than it is for 1 person to donate 10 pounds.
  • It's not just about the money - If a author has recieved 500 1p pledges they will be encourgaged to continue what they are doing.
  • Life is a marathon, not a sprint. Websites can benefit from a tiny trickle of small donations because they all add up.

Why should I use diddydonation.com?

  • Free service - no fees. We don't take a slice of the pie. We don't make a profit, not even 1p.
  • No commitment - Donations are IOUs rather than confirmed, i.e you can pledge a whole bunch of donations before you need to put your money where your mouth is. You're never committed to pay, unless you want to.
  • You can pledge to any website, you don't have to wait for the website to set up a donations box. Until they have given details on how to get funds to their bank, your dontations can only be IOUs.

Free? I don't believe you

  • OK - If you choose Paypal to transfer funds, you have to pay Paypal transfer fees. However, we offer payment via BACS which doesn't have any costs associated with it.
  • This website was free to build and is free to run, so why do we need to charge? I built it because I wanted to use a similar service but couldn't find anything open enough.

How do I give a larger donation?

There are plenty of other donations websites that accept normal donations:

  • Pledgie - Free except for Paypal fees
  • Justgiving - 5% fee
  • Everyclick - 4.8% on Credit and Debit card donations.
  • Chipin
  • Twitpay

How does payment work?

There is no commitment for you to make good your pledges. However, if you want to, you can choose two payment methods:

  1. You can pay the beneficiary directly using Paypal.
    • Advantages: you know your money is going straight to them.
    • Disadvantages: Paypal becomes a middleman and takes their processing fee (approx 20p + between 2-4%). There is also a question of delay. We recommend you don't donate less than £10 which means you will need to donate 1000 times to the same beneficiary. This might take a few years, or it might never happen.
  2. You can pay via BACS into the diddydonation bank account and we will act as a escrow.
    • Advantages: we can collect and aggregate small amounts from all our users to pay beneficiaries in larger or more frequent sums.
    • Disadvantages: You will be placing trust in us. Paying by BACS is a little inconvient compared to Paypal (and requires online banking).
